# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

Shirt Tracker is an open-source project built around respect, trust, collaboration, and inclusion. This Code of Conduct sets the standards for how contributors, maintainers, and community members are expected to interact while working on or around the project.

The goal is not only to prevent harm, but to build a community where people are treated with dignity, communicate clearly, and can participate without fear of harassment, exclusion, or retaliation.

## Who This Covers

This Code of Conduct applies to anyone participating in the Shirt Tracker project, including maintainers, contributors, reviewers, bug reporters, designers, and community members.

It applies in all project spaces, including:

- GitHub issues, pull requests, and code reviews
- Project documentation and discussions
- Direct project-related messages between participants
- Work connected to releases, testing, planning, and support
- Project-related social, community, or event spaces, whether online or in person

We welcome participation from people of different backgrounds, identities, and experiences. That includes, but is not limited to, differences in race, color, ethnicity, national origin, religion, sex, gender, gender identity, gender expression, sexual orientation, disability, age, marital status, veteran status, medical condition, socioeconomic background, and technical experience.

## Our Values

We expect project participants to:

- Treat people first, with empathy and professionalism
- Communicate respectfully, directly, and constructively
- Assume good intent where reasonable, while still addressing harmful impact
- Make space for people with different backgrounds, needs, and levels of experience
- Give feedback on ideas, code, and behavior without belittling the person behind them
- Protect privacy, trust, and confidential information
- Help build a project culture that is safe, welcoming, and accountable

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable in this project or in project-related spaces:

- Harassment, discrimination, or exclusionary behavior
- Bullying, intimidation, stalking, or sustained hostility
- Insults, slurs, degrading jokes, or demeaning language
- Sexualized comments, unwanted advances, or inappropriate sexual attention
- Threats of violence, encouragement of harm, or abusive conduct
- Publishing or threatening to publish private information without consent
- Retaliation against someone for raising a concern or participating in a review
- Deliberate disruption, bad-faith engagement, or sabotage of project work
- Repeated boundary-pushing after someone has asked for it to stop
- Inappropriate use of project spaces, assets, or communication channels
- Conduct at offsite or social events that creates an unsafe or exclusionary environment

Subtle behavior counts too. Belittling comments, dismissive patterns, stereotype-based remarks, or repeated "jokes" that make people feel unwelcome can still violate this Code of Conduct even when framed as informal or unintended.

## Reporting Concerns

If you experience or witness behavior that may violate this Code of Conduct, report it privately to the project maintainer:

- Aaron Dunn - `geniusaaron@me.com`

You may also use a private GitHub contact channel or another published project contact method.

If the concern involves the person you would normally contact, use another maintainer if available or use the platform's reporting and safety tools.

When making a report, include whatever information you can safely provide, such as:

- What happened
- Where and when it happened
- Who was involved
- Any links, screenshots, or other supporting context
- Whether you have immediate safety or retaliation concerns

Anonymous or limited-detail reports may still be reviewed to the extent possible.

## Response and Enforcement

Reports will be handled as promptly and fairly as practical. The person reviewing the report should:

- Acknowledge receipt
- Review the available information
- Avoid conflicts of interest where possible
- Explain the next steps and expected process
- Decide on an appropriate response based on the severity and pattern of behavior

Possible responses may include:

- A private warning or coaching conversation
- Removal of comments, posts, or other content
- Limits on participation in discussions or project spaces
- Temporary suspension from project participation
- Permanent removal from the project community
- Escalation to platform moderators or relevant authorities when necessary

Consequences may be stronger for repeated misconduct, abuse of power, retaliation, threats, or behavior that puts others at risk.

## Anti-Retaliation

Retaliation is prohibited. No one should be punished, intimidated, excluded, or threatened for making a good-faith report, participating in a review, supporting another person, or setting reasonable boundaries.

## Offsite and Social Conduct

This Code of Conduct also applies at project-related social events, meetups, conference interactions, chats, and other spaces connected to the project.

When organizing or participating in offsite interactions, contributors should help create inclusive conditions. That includes being mindful of venue accessibility, alcohol-related pressure, timing, caregiving responsibilities, and dietary or religious needs where relevant.

## Ongoing Review

This document should remain visible, easy to find, and subject to periodic review. A Code of Conduct is only effective when it is supported by consistent enforcement, reflection, and updates as the project grows.
